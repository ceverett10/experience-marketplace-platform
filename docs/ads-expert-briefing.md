## 1. What We've Built

We operate a **multi-tenant white-label marketplace** selling tours, activities, and attractions sourced from the Holibob wholesale API. The platform runs three types of sites simultaneously:

### Tier 1: Main Sites (Custom Domains)

22 live branded storefronts on custom domains — each focused on a specific niche or audience. Full branding, SEO content, and paid traffic capability.

### Tier 2: Opportunity Microsites

23 SEO-targeted microsites on `experiencess.com` subdomains (e.g. `kayaking-barcelona.experiencess.com`). Created from keyword research — each targets a high-value search term.

### Tier 3: Supplier Microsites

39,099 microsites on `experiencess.com` subdomains, one per supplier in the Holibob catalogue (e.g. `evan-evans-tours.experiencess.com`). Lighter-weight pages for SEO coverage.

## 4. Critical Issue: Zero Conversions

**£3,089 spent across both platforms with zero recorded bookings.**

Healthy CTRs (especially Google at 8–18%) confirm the ads themselves are reaching relevant audiences. The failure is happening **post-click**. Suspected causes:

### 4a. Conversion Tracking May Be Broken

The booking flow goes: our site → Holibob's checkout → payment confirmation. If the Google Ads / Meta conversion tag does not fire on the confirmation page (which may sit on a different domain/subdomain controlled by Holibob), zero conversions will be recorded even if bookings are completing. This is the most likely cause and must be verified first.

### 4b. Landing Page Experience Mismatch

Current landing page types:

- `EXPERIENCES_FILTERED` — `/experiences?q=keyword` page showing filtered results
- `DESTINATION` — a destination guide page (e.g. `/destinations/london`)
- `HOMEPAGE` — the site homepage

Users clicking a specific ad (e.g. "Harry Potter studio tour") may land on a filtered search page that shows many loosely related experiences rather than the exact product they expected. Competitor platforms (GetYourGuide, Viator) send paid traffic to individual product pages.

### 4c. Trust Deficit on New Domains

Most of these domains are recently registered (2024–2025). They have AI-generated content, limited reviews visible on the page, and no brand recognition. High-intent buyers for experiences typically need social proof to convert.

### 4d. Audience Signal Cold Start

With spend distributed across many campaigns, no single campaign has accumulated enough data (Google recommends 50+ conversions/month) for Smart Bidding to optimise. The bidding engine currently runs Manual CPC / Target ROAS — but Target ROAS without conversion data is effectively random.

## 5. Platform Architecture (Relevant to Ads)

### How the Bidding Engine Works

The platform has a custom-built bidding engine that runs autonomously:

1. **Keyword Discovery** — mines Google Search Console data for underperforming organic terms, expands from seed keywords, generates destination × category combinations. Enriches with Pinterest CPC estimates and Meta audience interest data.

2. **AI Quality Scoring** — Claude AI scores each keyword on 4 dimensions: relevance, commercial intent, competition viability, landing page fit. Keywords score BID (≥65), REVIEW (40-64), or SKIP (<40).

3. **Profitability Model** — `maxCPC = (AOV × CVR × commission) / targetROAS`
   Current defaults: AOV £264, CVR 1.5%, commission 18%, targetROAS 1.0
   These are estimated defaults — not derived from actual booking data.

4. **Landing Page Routing** — selects the most relevant page type for each keyword via a priority ladder:
   Homepage (branded) → Blog (informational) → Collection → Destination → Category → Filtered experiences

5. **Campaign Deployment** — creates campaigns via Google Ads API and Meta Graph API directly. Google uses RSA (Responsive Search Ads) with AI-generated headlines and descriptions.

6. **Bidding** — campaigns start on Manual CPC, migrate to Target ROAS after 15+ conversions (none have reached this threshold).

### Google Ads Structure

- Account type: Single MCC with sub-accounts per site
- Campaign type: Standard Search (STAG pattern)
- Ad format: Responsive Search Ads (3 headlines, 2 descriptions minimum)
- Keywords: Phrase match
- Ad extensions: Sitelinks, callouts, structured snippets auto-attached
- Geo: Presence or interest (not presence-only)
- **Not currently using**: Performance Max, Google Things to Do (GTtD/Travel campaigns)

### Meta Ads Structure

- Campaign type: CBO (Campaign Budget Optimisation)
- Targeting: Interest-based (supplier names, activity categories)
- Ad format: Single image + copy (AI-generated)
- Creative: Auto-generated from Holibob product images
- **DSA compliance** applied for EU audiences

### What Is Not Yet Implemented

- **Google Things to Do** — the dominant format used by GetYourGuide and Viator (feed-based campaigns that show individual tour listings in search results). Requires connectivity partner approval or listing through an approved OTA.
- **Performance Max** — not yet running alongside Search campaigns
- **Microsoft Ads** — not connected
- **Remarketing / retargeting** — no audience lists built yet
- **Dynamic Search Ads** — not configured

## 8. Key Questions for the Ads Expert

1. **Conversion tracking** — how do we correctly attribute bookings that complete on a Holibob-hosted checkout page back to our Google/Meta campaigns? Is cross-domain tracking sufficient or do we need server-side conversion API?

2. **Landing page strategy** — should we send paid traffic to individual experience product pages rather than filtered search results? How do we best match ad intent to landing page?

3. **Campaign structure** — is STAG still the right approach given Google's push toward broad match + Smart Bidding? Should we consolidate campaigns to accelerate conversion data accumulation?

4. **Google Things to Do** — how do we get our experiences into GTtD feed-based campaigns? Is the right route to list through Viator/GYG as a distribution channel, or to apply directly via Google Actions Center?

5. **Trust signals** — what additions to landing pages would most improve conversion rate for cold paid traffic on new domains?

6. **Budget allocation** — given £0 conversions, should we pause all broad campaigns and focus 100% of budget on the highest-intent / most established site (Harry Potter Tours) until we prove conversion works?

7. **Meta vs Google** — Meta spend (£2,064) has produced lower CTR and no conversions vs Google (£1,025, 8%+ CTR). Is Meta the right channel for booking-intent traffic or is it better suited to retargeting?
